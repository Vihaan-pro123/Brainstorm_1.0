1. A student recorded the mass of dry raisins as 20g and the mass of raisins after soaking as 35g. Calculate the percentage of water absorbed by the raisins? Why did the raisins absorb water?

2. What are the consequences of the following conditions?

(a) A cell having higher water concentration than the surrounding medium.

(b) A cell having lower water concentration than the surrounding medium.

(c) A cell having equal water concentration to its surrounding medium.

3. A student prepared two temporary mounts, one of onion peel and another of cheek cells but forgot to label the slides. Which of the following observations will help in identifying the cheek cells slide?
(a) Presence of cell membrane
(b) Absence of cell wall
(c) Presence of nucleus
(d) Absence of intercellular space between the adjacent cells

4. Which of the following is found both in the cell of a mango plant and a monkey?
(a) cell wall
b) chloroplasts
(c) cell membrane
(d) both chloroplasts and cell membrane

5. In which type of cell are plastids present? Name the two types of plastids. Write one difference between the two types of plastids.

6. Why do people sprinkle water on the open ground or roof top on a hot sunny day?

7. When you add sugar to a glass of water, the sugar disappears without any change in the volume. Which property of matter does this observation show?

(a) 38
(b) -20

9. At what temperature liquid water and steam co-exists?
(a) 273.15K
(b) 373.15K
(c) 473.15K
(d) 173.15K

10. A few substances are arranged in the increasing order of ‘forces of attraction’ between their particles. Which one of the following is the correct arrangement?
(a) oxygen – honey – sugar
(b) oil – air – salt
c) juice – salt – oxygen
(d) salt – air – sugar solution

11. A speed-time graph represents –
(a) acceleration
(b) speed
(c) displacement
(d) distance

12. A car travel with a velocity 10 ms-1 with uniform acceleration of 5ms-2. Calculate the final velocity when it has travelled 30 m.

13. A car travels from stop A to stop B with a speed of 30 km/h and then returns back to A with a speed of 50 km/h. If the distance between A and B is 30 km. Find-
(a) Displacement of the car.
(b) Distance travelled by the car.
(c) Average speed of the car.
